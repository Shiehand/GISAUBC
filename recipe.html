Tahu Goreng Perkedel

Difficulty:
Spiciness:
When in need of a savory snack or a side dish for your meal time, Tahu Goreng Perkedel can be your go to menu. Itâ€™s easy to make using the staples in your kitchen! The crispy outside from eggs and flour with the soft inside from the tofu will make you want more!
  • 3 soft tofu
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 shallots, minced
  • 2 stems of scallions
  • 2 eggs
  • mushroom powder, to taste
  • salt, to taste
  • pepper, to taste
  • vegetable oil
  • 3 tahu
  • 3 bawang putih, dicincang
  • 3 bawang merah, dicincang
  • 2 daun bawang
  • 2 telur
  • 2 sdm tepung
  • bumbu jamur
  • garam
  • lada
  • minyak goreng

Instructions

  1. Using a spoon, break down the tofu into smaller pieces.
  2. Heat up the oil.
  3. Mince all the garlic, shallots and scallions and combine them with the tofu into the bowl.
  4. Add 2 tbsp of flour, 2 eggs, salt, pepper and mushroom powder to taste in the bowl, mix.
  5. Using a spoon, scoop out the mixture and deep fry them.
  6. Flip them when they turn brown and remove tofu and drain on a wire rack to remove excess oil.
  7. Serve with hot rice and sweet soy sauce!

Pisang Goreng

Difficulty:
Spiciness:
Pisang goreng, sweet and crispy when it is done right! Getting the right bananas for this dish in Vancouver might not be easy, but when craving for some, we try to make it work! The perfect companion for your me time.
  • 8 ripe saba bananas or raja bananas
  • 100 grams rice flour
  • 1.5 tsp baking soda
  • 1.5 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 0.5 tsp salt
  • Water
  • Oil
  • 8 pisang kepok atau pisang raja
  • 100 gram tepung beras
  • 1.5 sdt soda kue
  • 1.5 sdt bubuk pengembang
  • 2 sdt gula
  • 0.5 sdt garam
  • Air
  • Minyak goreng

Instructions

  1. Peel all the bananas. Optionally, make three slits along its length (but two will also be enough), but keep the bottom 1 inch intact, so the bananas can be opened up like a fan.
  2. In a separate bowl add in all the rice flour, baking soda, baking powder, sugar and mix everything
  3. Once the oil is hot (you will see bubbles), scoop 1 tablespoon of hot oil and add to the batter, whisk again.
  4. Coat the bananas with batter and deep-fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes.
  5. At around 2 minutes into frying, drop batter in drips into the hot oil to make plenty of batter droplets, then quickly coat the bananas with the resulting crispy bits. This creates an additional layer to ensure your resulting pisang goreng stays crispy for longer.
  6. Remove bananas and drain on a wire rack to remove excess oil. Pisang goreng is best when served immediately.
